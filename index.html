About Me

Marco is a Postdoctoral Research Associate at the University of Cambridge. He is engaged in collaborative research at the Departments of Medicine and Engineering as a member of the ADVANTAGE consortium for visceral pain. He is also an Associate Lecturer at the Open University, where he teaches Nanoscale Engineering and The Engineering Project. He has been a Postdoctoral By-Fellow at Churchill College, University of Cambridge, since 2023.

Marco was born and raised in Quito, Ecuador, where he earned his Engineering Degree in Mechatronics from the University of the Armed Forces in 2013. He moved to South Korea to pursue his graduate studies in 2014 and was chosen for the Korean Government Scholarship Program (now known as the Global Korea Scholarship). He graduated with a Master of Engineering in Micro/Nano Systems from Korea University in 2018 and worked under Prof. Seunghyup Yoo's direction to complete his Doctorate of Philosophy in Electrical Engineering from KAIST in 2022.

In 2022, Marco relocated to the UK to work as a Postdoctoral Research Associate at the University of Cambridge. Marco's research focuses on the intersection of electronics and medicine, specifically the role of skin-interfacing electronics and wearables in living systems, with the goal of developing advanced tools for healthcare using bioelectronic sensors, low-power electronics, and artificial intelligence.
