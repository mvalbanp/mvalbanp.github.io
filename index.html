Marco Vinicio Alban-Paccha

Postdoctoral Research Associate, Department of Medicine and Department of Engineering, University of Cambridge, UK


Marco is a Postdoctoral Research Associate at the University of Cambridge, he is working jointly at the Department of Medicine and the Department of Engineering as part of the ADVANTAGE consortium for visceral pain. He is also an Associate Lecturer at both Open University and Universidad de las Americas, where he lectures Nanoscale Engineering and Artificial Intelligence respectively. Starting on 2023, he will join Churchill College, University of Cambridge as a Postdoctoral By-Fellow.

Marco was born and raised in Quito, Ecuador, where he received his Engineering Degree in Mechatronics from the University of the Armed Forces in 2013. He was selected for the Korean Government Scholarship Program (now Global Korea Scholarship) in 2014, year when he moved to South Korea to pursue his Graduate studies. He received his Master in Engineering Degree in Micro/Nano Systems from Korea University in 2018 and he completed his Doctor of Philosophy degree in Electrical Engineering in 2022 under the guidance of Prof. Seunghyup Yoo.

Marco moved to the United Kingdom in 2022 in order to join the University of Cambridge as a Postdoctoral Research Associate. Marco's research focuses in the intersection between electronics and medicine, particularly about the role of skin-interfacing electronics and wearables in living systems, in order to develop advanced tools for healthcare with the use of low-power electronics, electrodes and Artificial Intelligence.
